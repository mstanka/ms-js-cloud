Moving to Cloud — my personal perspective after consulting with 100+ companies

Published on 12 May 2019

Cloud is everywhere today. People use it even if they don’t realize it. There are over 2 billion active users on Facebook, over 1 billion active users on Instagram, 300 million users on Amazon. And so on.

On the other hand, a lot of companies I work with are cautious of cloud computing. They even think they don’t need it. I advise to all sorts of companies — local, global, multinational, national, private, public. Slowly some of them start to consider using cloud platforms for their minor workloads.

By the way, it’s fun to see how they make small steps on their way to cloud. It’s like watching babies learning to walk.

When they start using cloud services, they want to use them in the same way they use (or provide) “services” in their data centers.

Until today I remember how excited I was about virtualization when I started using it in 2007. Soon afterwards, I started to develop my own private cloud (that name was not widely used at that time). It had most of the key cloud characteristics:

  • on-demand self-service — via custom developed management portal
  • resource pooling — our computing resources were pooled to serve multiple consumers
  • elasticity — capabilities could be elastically provisioned and released
